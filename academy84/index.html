Curriculum — Introduction to Cybersecurity

12 units. 48 modules. Everything covered.

Each unit builds on the last. By unit 12 you'll have a complete picture of how security works — and why it matters in a business context.

1

Cybersecurity Fundamentals

CIA Triad, AAA, threat actors, risk mechanics, least privilege, Zero Trust

2

Networking Fundamentals

OSI model, TCP/IP, ports and DNS, switches vs routers, firewalls

3

Principles and Concepts

Security as business insurance, control types — preventive, detective, corrective, deterrent

4

Threats and Vulnerabilities

Zero-days, malware types, social engineering, OWASP Top 10, SQL injection

5

Identity and Access

Zero Trust, MFA factors, JML lifecycle, privilege creep, MAC/RBAC/ABAC/DAC

6

Security Operations

SOC, SIEM, alert fatigue, incident response lifecycle

7

Cryptography

Symmetric vs asymmetric, hashing, PKI, TLS and HTTPS

8

Network Security

Defence in depth, DMZ, VPNs, IDS vs IPS, network segmentation

9

Endpoint and Application Security

Patch management, hardening, EDR, malware protection

10

Policies and Governance

Policy hierarchy, AUP, CISO role, risk lifecycle, shadow IT

11

BCDR

3-2-1 rule, backup types, immutable backups, RPO/RTO, DRP testing

12

Final Prep

ISC2 CC exam technique, manager mindset, Cyber Essentials 5 pillars, career pathways
